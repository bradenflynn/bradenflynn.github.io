Case Study: Toyota Pricing

Toyota Used Car Pricing Model

What exactly makes a used Toyota more expensive?

01. The Goal

In this project, I analyzed a dataset of 6,738 used Toyota vehicles to figure out the most important factors that drive up a car's price. My objective was to build a tool that helps a buyer or seller quickly estimate a fair price based on simple specs like engine size and age.

6,738 vehicle records containing mileage, fuel type, engine size, and model year.

A predictive formula that estimates car value with a quantified "confidence score."

02. How I Got the Numbers

1. Fixing "Impossible" Data

Data is rarely perfect. During the cleaning phase, I found cars listed with over 200 MPG—which isn't physically possible for these models! I researched the correct manufacturer specs and fixed these errors to ensure the final model wasn't skewed by "dirty" data.

# The Formula

"For every 1 liter of engine size added, the car's price jumps by ~$10,673."

2. Finding the Trend (Linear Regression)

I utilized a technique called "Linear Regression." Imagine drawing a straight line through a cloud of data points to find the average path. This line told us the exact dollar value of engine power: every 1-liter increase in engine size correlates to roughly a $10.6k increase in price.

3. The Confidence Score (R²)

How much can we trust this model? I calculated the "Rnd-Squared" value, which I call the Confidence Score. It came out to 53%. This means that half of a car's price variation is determined just by its engine size, while the other half is things like condition, color, and location.
